SwipeClean Privacy Policy

Effective date: 2026-06-12

SwipeClean is an on-device photo cleaning app. This policy explains what data the app accesses, what data leaves the device, and how users can contact support.

Developer

SwipeClean is provided by the seller or developer account listed for SwipeClean on the App Store. The support and privacy contact for SwipeClean is listed in the Contact section below.

Summary

SwipeClean does not operate a backend service and does not upload photos or videos. Photo analysis, swipe history, delete queue state, daily free swipe quota, and month progress are processed and stored locally on the user's device.

Data We Collect

SwipeClean does not collect, transmit, sell, rent, or share user data with the developer or third parties.

The app may access photos and videos, local app settings, StoreKit entitlement state, and local notification permission state on the user's device solely to provide app functionality. These items are processed on device and are not sent to SwipeClean, a server, analytics provider, advertising network, or data broker.

Photos and Videos

SwipeClean uses Apple's PhotoKit and Vision frameworks to review the user's library, estimate storage savings, identify screenshots, large videos, similar photos, duplicates, and blurry photos, and queue items selected for deletion.

Deleting photos is performed through Apple's Photos framework and the system confirmation flow. Deleted photos may remain in the user's Recently Deleted album for the period controlled by Apple Photos.

Purchases

Paid features are sold through Apple's in-app purchase system. Payment processing is handled by Apple. SwipeClean does not receive or store payment card information.

Notifications

SwipeClean may ask for permission to schedule local reminders. These notifications are scheduled on the user's device. SwipeClean does not use a push notification server.

Third Parties and Tracking

SwipeClean does not include third-party analytics SDKs, advertising SDKs, social SDKs, crash reporting SDKs, tracking SDKs, or data broker integrations. SwipeClean does not track users across apps or websites and does not use the IDFA.

Data Retention and Deletion

SwipeClean stores local app state on the user's device. The user can remove this local state by deleting the app. Photos selected for deletion are handled by Apple Photos and can be managed in the Photos app, including the Recently Deleted album where applicable.

Changes

If SwipeClean's data practices change, this policy and App Store privacy disclosures will be updated before the changed version is submitted.
